You wrote an agent that signs Solana transactions. It works in your tests. Now ship it.

Altheia is the layer between your agent and the chain. Register the agent, attach a per-action policy, and the SDK checks every signing call against your caps and program allowlist before it runs. Pause from the dashboard and the agent stops mid-loop. Revoke and the keypair is dead.

## What the SDK gives you

| Surface | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| `altheia.guard(action, fn)` | Pre-flight check, run, audit. One wrap, three guarantees. |
| Per-asset caps | Max per tx and max per day, in human units. |
| Program allowlist | The agent can only invoke programs you whitelisted. |
| Pause / revoke | One click in the dashboard. Pause is reversible. Revoke kills the session key. |
| Audit trail | Every allow and deny, anchored hourly to Solana via Merkle root. |

## How the trust story actually works

The agent's keypair is generated **in your browser** when you register. The backend never sees the secret. If our database is compromised tomorrow, nobody can sign as your agent.

Policy is enforced in two places. The SDK pre-flight catches denials off-chain before any fee is paid. Swig's session-key scope enforces it on-chain as the floor. The off-chain layer is fast. The on-chain layer is the one you trust.
